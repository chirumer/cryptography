\begin{frame}{Maximal Gain Attack (MGA) on kRR, OUE, and OLH}
\small

\textbf{Idea:} For each fake user, choose a reported value \(y\) that maximizes
\[
\sum_{t \in T} \mathbf{1}_{S(y)}(t),
\]
where \(T\) is the set of target items and \(S(y)\) is the support set of \(y\).

\medskip
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{1.7cm} p{8.7cm}}
\toprule
Protocol & MGA mechanism for each fake user \\
\midrule
kRR &
Domain \(D = [d]\), support \(S(y) = \{y\}\). \\
& Maximize \(\sum_{t\in T}\mathbf{1}_{S(y)}(t)\le 1\), so choose any target:
fake user \emph{always reports} a target item \(y \in T\). \\[0.3em]
\midrule
OUE &
Domain \(D = \{0,1\}^d\), support \(S(y) = \{v : y_v = 1\}\). \\
& Maximize \(\sum_{t\in T}\mathbf{1}_{S(y)}(t)\le |T|\) by setting
\(y_t = 1\) for all \(t \in T\). \\
& To mimic genuine users, also set \(l \approx p + (d-1)q - |T|\)
\emph{additional nonâ€“target bits} to 1, so the total number of 1s
matches the expected number for honest users. \\[0.3em]
\midrule
OLH &
Domain \(D = \{(H,a)\}\), support \(S(H,a) = \{v : H(v)=a\}\). \\
& Maximize \(\sum_{t\in T}\mathbf{1}_{S(H,a)}(t)\le |T|\) by choosing
a hash function \(H\) and bucket \(a\) such that many targets collide:
\(H(t)=a\) for \(t\in T\). \\
& In theory: pick \(H\) with \(H(t)=a\) for all \(t\in T\); in practice:
search/sampler over hash functions and use the one that maps the
largest number of targets to the same \(a\). \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
